Bears

Bear Facts (link)

Bears are omnivorous (sometimes carnivorous) animals that can weigh anywhere from 110 to 1,600 pounds.
They can be found in continents such as North and South America, Asia, and etc.
Their smell and agility are impeccable, despite their poor hearing and sight.

Giraffes

  1. Frank
  2. Coconut

Giraffe Facts (link)

Giraffes are tall mammals, found in Africa's open woodlands.
They are herbivores and often snack on trees, which is made easy by their infamously long necks.
Adding onto the giraffes weird anatomy is their strange dark blue tongues and cartilage horns.

Lions

  1. Mella
  2. Karl

Lion Facts (link)

Lions are large cats (second in size to tigers), that live in groups of up to 40.
They primarily hunt animals such as zebra, antelope, and gazelle.
These animal have no natural enemies or predators, making them even more intimidating.

Monkey Facts (link)

Monkeys are intelligent primates that have long legs and arms, with which they use both to move around.
They normally have flat faces, narrow chests, and hair around most of their bodies.

Alligator Facts (link)

Alligators are large reptiles that can range anywhere from 10 to 15 feet in length.
These animals are meant for the water, which is proven by their webbed feet and tails that
account for half of their size.
